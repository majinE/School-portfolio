Project 2: Gun Control: A Literature Review

Mass Shootings

In her article, “These are the 10 deadliest mass shootings in modern U.S. history,” author Ciara Linnane lists the tragedies with their motives, that have provided the biggest influence on today’s political gun debate:

Las Vegas: Oct. 1, 2017. 58 killed and more than 515 wounded. Shooter: 64-year old Stephen Paddock. Motive: Unknown. Orlando, Fla.: June 12, 2016. 49 killed and more than 58 wounded. Shooter: 29-year-old Omar Mateen. Motive: terror, hate crime. Blacksburg, Va.: April 16, 2007. 33 killed … and 17 wounded on the campus of Virginia Tech. Shooter: 23-year-old Seung-Hui Cho. Motive: Mental-health issues. Newton, Conn.: Dec. 14, 2012. 28 killed, including shooter and his mother along with 20 children … at Sandy Hook Elementary School. Shooter: 20-year-old Adam Lanza. Motive: Mental-health issues. Killeen, Texas: Oct. 16, 1991. 24 killed and 27 wounded in a massacre at a Luby’s cafeteria. Shooter: 35-year-old George Hennard. Motive: Hatred of women, ethnic minorities. San Ysidro, CA.: July 19, 1984. 22 killed and 19 wounded. Shooter: 41-year-old James Huberty. Motive: Mental-health issues. Austin, Texas: Aug. 1, 1966. 18 killed and 31 wounded. Shooter: 25-year-old Charles Whitman. Motive: Mental-health issues, brain tumor was found after his death. Edmond, Okla.: Aug. 20, 1986. 15 killed and 6 wounded. Shooter: 44-year-old postal worker Patrick Sherill. Motive: Revenge after a workplace supervisory reprimand. San Bernardino, CA.: Dec. 2, 2015. 16 killed and 24 wounded. Shooters: 28-year-old Syed Rizwan Farook and wife, Tashfeen Malik. Motive: Terror after shooter were radicalized online and committed to jihadism. Fort Hood, Texas: Nov 5, 2009. 13 killed and 33 wounded. Shooter: Nidal Hasan. Motive: Uncertain, shooter and his family have described it as terror inspired by radical Islam.

Conclusion

The gun control debate is a popular modern controversial topic, circulating throughout our country’s politics. Statistics and research used for both sides of the controversy made it slightly difficult to choose a political side on the issue. There seems to be unmistakable evidence that establishing more laws regulating gun ownership and market results in pros and cons. Strangely enough, the recent government release on the classified “JFK files” are what has set my opinion on the matter. I’m curious on whether evidence clearly stating that government agencies have developed plans to terrorize their own people, lie, and point to and blame others for the responsibility, can decimate the liberal claim that the citizens should fully depend on government for protection. It opens questions to whether the Federal Government still abide to their purpose set by the preamble; establish Justice, insure domestic Tranquility, and provide for the common defense. Our government has created plans to torment their people through violent means; should Americans give up their only right that allows citizens to rebel against their government if they begin to tyrannize the people?
